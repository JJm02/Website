About Labuan Bird Park

Labuan Bird Park is a serene and lush wildlife sanctuary located in Labuan, Malaysia. Home to a diverse range of bird species such as hornbills, eagles, flamingos, peacocks, and various tropical birds native to Southeast Asia, the park offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience the region's rich avian biodiversity. Its large walk-in aviaries and beautifully landscaped surroundings provide a natural and immersive environment for both the birds and guests.

In addition to its vibrant birdlife, the park features educational exhibits and guided tours aimed at raising awareness about bird conservation and environmental protection. It is a popular attraction for families, tourists, and bird lovers who come to learn, explore, and capture stunning photographs. Whether you're interested in discovering exotic species or simply enjoying a peaceful walk in nature, Labuan Bird Park promises a colorful and memorable experience.

Labuan Bird Park stands out as the only bird park in Borneo and is managed entirely by the Department of Veterinary Services under Malaysia’s Ministry of Agriculture and Agro-based Industry. Situated in the northern region of Labuan, the park is about 16 kilometers from the city center, requiring roughly 20 minutes by car to reach.

This bird park is home to a wide collection of exotic and native bird species. Its development started in 1995, with construction finishing in 1997. Although it welcomed visitors starting in 1999, the park was officially inaugurated on August 4, 2003, by YB Dato’ Seri Haji Mohd Sharif bin Haji Omar, who was the Deputy Minister of Agriculture and Agro-based Industry at the time.

Spanning an area of 6.9 hectares, the park features three large domes designed as aviaries and is surrounded by lush, well-maintained landscaping. It serves as a refuge for various bird species native to Borneo, often observed and recorded by bird enthusiasts. To enhance the visitor experience, the park includes amenities such as a walk-in aviary, bird sanctuary, gazebos, rest areas, food stalls, walking paths, a souvenir shop, a children's playground, and a designated prayer room.
